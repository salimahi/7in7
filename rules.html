Competition Rules

The Rules

Everything you need to know before you write your script. Read it once. Then get to work.

Schedule

Competition Cycle

The same calendar repeats every month. All times are Eastern Time (ET).

Phase Date / Timing Details
Prompt Release 7pm ET on the 7th The monthly writing prompt is announced. Your 7-day window begins.
Prompt & Link Delivered 7pm ET on the 7th Registered entrants receive one email containing the prompt and their unique single-use submission link.
Writing Window 7th – 14th 7 days to write a 7-page script in PDF format.
Submission Deadline 7pm ET on the 14th Scripts submitted after this time will not be accepted.
Judging & Feedback 15th – 21st Judges score and write individual feedback for every submission.
Results & Feedback Delivery By the 22nd Winners announced. All entrants receive their written feedback.
Table Read Published Within 30 days of results The winning script is produced and published as a podcast episode.

Requirements

Script Requirements

📄

Up to 7 Pages

Your script must be no more than 7 pages, not counting any title page. Scripts longer than 7 pages are automatically disqualified. Fewer than 7 pages is allowed.

📋

PDF Format

Submit your script as a PDF file. The file must open and be readable. No other formats are accepted.

🎬

Standard Screenplay Format

Your script must follow standard screenplay format: sluglines, action lines, and dialogue blocks. Format is one of the scored categories.

💡

Prompt Engagement

Your script must engage with the monthly prompt in a meaningful way — not just literally. The lead judge confirms this during review.

✍️

Original Work

Submissions must be original, written solely by the entrant during the 7-day writing window. No AI-generated content.

🕵️

Anonymous Submission

Your PDF must contain no identifying information. If included, a title page should feature the script title only — no author name. Judging is blind.

1️⃣

One Script Per Cycle

Each entrant may submit one script per monthly cycle. Each entry fee covers one submission.

©️

Your Script, Your Rights

Your submitted work remains your intellectual property. Entering grants us a limited license to share excerpts with you as part of your feedback, and to potentially produce your script as a table read podcast episode. All other rights remain entirely with you.

Eligibility

Disqualification Gates

Before scoring begins, every submission passes through four gates. Failing any gate means your script is ineligible for prizes — but you still receive written feedback.

1
File Readable The script opens and is in a recognized format (PDF). Corrupted or password-protected files are rejected.
2
Page Count Script is no more than 7 pages, excluding any title page. Scripts exceeding 7 pages are disqualified. Fewer pages are permitted.
3
Prompt Engagement Script engages with the monthly prompt in a meaningful way. Confirmed by the lead judge — not a programmatic check.
4
Minimum Score Threshold To be eligible for a prize, the script must achieve a total judge score of at least 7 out of 35.

Judging

The Judging Rubric

Every eligible script is scored across 7 categories on a scale of −5 to +5. Zero is true neutral: the script meets basic expectations without distinction. Scores are used for ranking only and are not shared with entrants. All entrants receive written feedback referencing each category.

Scoring Scale

+5
Exceptional. A clear strength that would stand out in any competition.
+3 to +4
Above average. Solid craft with notable moments.
+1 to +2
Slightly above neutral. Works, but not memorable.
0
Neutral. Competent but generic. Does the job, nothing more.
−1 to −2
Slightly below neutral. Problems present but not fatal.
−3 to −4
Significant weakness. Undermines the script.
−5
Fails this category. A core problem that must be addressed.

The 7 Scored Categories

Category 01

Format

Standard screenplay format: sluglines, action lines, dialogue blocks. At 7 pages, sloppiness is a choice, not a space constraint.

Category 02

Premise & Use of Prompt

Is the central idea clear? Does it use the prompt in a meaningful (not just literal) way?

Category 03

Structure

Does the story have a shape? Setup, turn, resolution. Pacing appropriate for 7 pages.

Category 04

Character

At least one character feels specific and motivated. Change or stasis is intentional.

Category 05

Dialogue

Sounds human, does work, avoids on-the-nose exposition.

Category 06

Visual Storytelling

Written for the camera. Story told through action and image, not just words.

Category 07

Resonance

Does it land? Does something stick after the read?

Total score range: −35 to +35. Prize eligibility requires a minimum score of 7 out of 35. Written feedback must address categories 2 through 5 directly.
