Frequently Asked Questions

Got Questions?

Everything writers ask before they enter — answered.

About the Competition

What is 7 in 7?

7 in 7 is a monthly online screenwriting competition for short film writers. Each month, a prompt is released on the 7th and you have 7 days to write a 7-page script. Every entrant receives written feedback from experienced judges. Cash prizes and a produced podcast table read go to the top three finishers.

Who can enter?

7 in 7 is open to writers worldwide, aged 18 or older, at any experience level. Whether you've written one script or a hundred, the competition is built for you. Employees, contractors, and immediate family members of the organizers are not eligible to enter.

Is this competition related to any other screenwriting competition?

7 in 7 is the spiritual successor to a 5-pages-in-5-days competition that ran successfully until the organizer stepped away. We've kept what worked — international, open to all levels, real feedback, low entry fee — and added a produced podcast table read for the winner.

Entry & Payment

How do I enter?

Pay the entry fee ($25 per cycle or $240 for an annual subscription). Upon payment confirmation, you'll receive a unique, single-use submission link via email. Use that link to upload your PDF script before 7pm ET on the 14th of the competition month. Submissions without a valid link will not be accepted.

What is the annual subscription?

The annual subscription is $240/year, billed upfront, and covers all 12 monthly cycles in a calendar year — equivalent to $20 per cycle instead of $25. You'll receive a unique submission link each month automatically. Subscriptions are non-refundable.

Can I submit more than one script per cycle?

No. Each entry fee — whether pay-per-cycle or one month of an annual subscription — covers one script per competition cycle. Each entrant may submit one script per month.

Are entry fees refundable?

Entry fees and subscription fees are non-refundable, except as provided for withheld prizes in cases where the minimum score threshold is not met by any entrant in a given cycle. See the Terms & Conditions for details.

Script & Format

What format must my script be in?

Your script must be submitted as a PDF file in standard screenplay format — sluglines, action lines, and dialogue blocks. It must be exactly 7 pages, not counting a title page. Formatting is one of the 7 scored categories, so take it seriously.

Does my script have to be about the prompt directly?

Your script must engage with the prompt in a meaningful way, as determined by the lead judge. "Meaningful" does not mean literal — you don't have to center the prompt word-for-word. But a script that ignores the prompt entirely will be disqualified. Creative interpretation is encouraged; prompts are a starting point, not a cage.

Can I submit a previously written script?

No. Submissions must be original work written solely by the entrant during the 7-day competition window. Previously published or submitted scripts are not eligible.

Can I use AI to write my script?

No. Submissions containing AI-generated content are subject to disqualification. The competition is for writers, and the feedback is designed to help you grow as one.

Judging & Feedback

How is judging done?

All eligible submissions are judged by qualified judges selected by the organizers. Scripts are scored across 7 categories on a scale of −5 to +5. Judges score each category independently before reviewing others. Judging decisions are final.

Will I see my scores?

No. Scores are used for ranking only and are not disclosed to entrants. What you receive is written feedback addressing each of the 7 scored categories — which is more useful than a number.

How is feedback delivered?

Written feedback is delivered to every entrant by the 22nd of the competition month, via email. This applies to all entrants — including those whose scripts were disqualified from prize eligibility. If you entered and wrote your 7 pages, you get feedback.

What happens if my script is disqualified?

Disqualified scripts are ineligible for prizes, but you still receive written feedback. Disqualification occurs when a script fails one of the four pre-scoring gates: file readability, page count, prompt engagement, or minimum score threshold. See the Rules page for details.

Prizes & Payment

How are prizes paid?

Prizes are delivered via Tremendous virtual Visa card. Winners must provide valid payment details within 30 days of notification. Unclaimed prizes are forfeited. Tax documentation (W-9 for US winners, W-8BEN for international winners) is required before payment is issued.

Can I win multiple times across cycles?

Yes. There is no limit on how many times an entrant may win across different competition cycles.

I'm an international writer. Is there a prize tax withholding?

Yes. US law requires 7 in 7 to withhold 30% of prize value before payment to international winners, unless the winner's country of residence has a tax treaty with the US that provides a reduced rate. A W-8BEN must be completed before payment is issued. The withheld amount is remitted to the IRS and a Form 1042-S is provided to the winner. Winners from treaty countries may claim a reduced rate on their W-8BEN. US winners receive prizes gross and are responsible for reporting prize income themselves.

The Podcast Table Read

What is the podcast table read?

Each month's winning script is produced as a performed table read and published as an episode of the 7 in 7 podcast, distributed across all major streaming platforms. Voice actors are recruited from the Richmond film and theater community. Recording is done virtually. The goal is to give the winner a real, shareable, portfolio-ready artifact — not just a title.

Does winning require me to participate in the table read?

No. The winner is invited to participate (as a voice actor, narrator, or character role), but participation is optional and does not affect the prize. If you're available on the recording date and want to be involved, great. If not, your script still gets produced.

Who owns my script?

You do — always. Your script is your intellectual property before, during, and after the competition, regardless of how you place.

By entering, every participant grants 7 in 7 a limited, non-exclusive license to reproduce excerpts of their script for feedback purposes, and to potentially produce their script as a table read podcast episode. No other rights are transferred. You remain free to publish, sell, adapt, or submit your script anywhere else.

When is the podcast published?

The organizers will make reasonable efforts to produce and publish the winning script as a podcast episode within 30 days of results being announced.
